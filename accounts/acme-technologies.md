## Pain & Business Case

**Primary business pain:**
ACME has grown rapidly through acquisitions and organic hiring, leaving them with 3 separate knowledge systems (Confluence, an internal wiki, and a legacy intranet from an acquired company). Engineers and support reps report spending 30–45 minutes per day searching for information across systems. New hires are taking 60+ days to ramp. The CTO has made "unified knowledge and AI" a Q1 initiative.

**Secondary pain points:**
- Support team is fielding 200+ repetitive internal IT/HR how-to tickets per month — equivalent to 1.5 FTEs in wasted time
- Leadership is explicitly planning to not hire 2 support FTEs this year if Glean can deflect internal tickets
- Sales team doesn't have easy access to the latest product docs, pricing, and competitive positioning — reps are going rogue with outdated materials

**Compelling event:**
Alex Chen's Q1 AI mandate — he has a board-level commitment to deploy an AI productivity platform by Q1 end. Missing this deadline would be embarrassing for him personally and would push any decision to Q2 at earliest.

**Urgency level:** High — Q1 deadline, budget already allocated, headcount plan built around Glean being in place

**Quantified business case:**
- Productivity savings: 2,100 employees × 12 min/day × 235 working days × $65/hr loaded cost = **$1.06M/year**
- Support headcount avoided: 2 FTEs × $120k fully loaded = **$240k/year**
- Tool consolidation (Guru + internal wiki + legacy intranet licenses): **$180k/year**
- **Total first-year value: ~$1.48M vs. $500k investment = 3x ROI**

**Their definition of success (6 months post-deployment):**
- Engineer ramp time reduced from 60 days to 30 days
- Internal support tickets down 40%
- >70% of employees actively using Glean weekly
- Sales team using Glean for account research and call prep
